How it Works

It is a network of interoperable, scalable blockchain shards that utilizes DPoS to elect 21 Block Producers for each shard who compete for the coin holders vote by offering them the largest and most reliable cloud resources.

By taking the best from EOS and Ethereum, like DPoS, Solidity, sharding, real decentralization and staking, Nominis offers a state-of-the-art platform to launch and scale a dApp in a sufficiently decentralized manner

Decentralization is guaranteed by scaling horizontally among many shards, thus allowing even home PCs to participate in the consensus validation. On the other hand, it is really easy for Ethereum dApps developer to switch because Nominis smart contracts are written in both Solidity and Javascript.

Features

01

Validatable decentra

Unlike EOS and many other advertised-to-be-super-scalable monolith blockchain networks, home PCs can participate in the validation of the consensus inside the side-chain they are interested in.

02

Smart contracts dApp

dApps in Nominis are programmed in Solidity and are run on an eWASM-like VM, very similar to the one developed by the Ethereum team to replace the old world-state VM

03

Horizontal scalabili

In a cluster of networks, where each network operates in a blockchain-backed consensus in order to execute the smart contracts logic, Nominis maintains interoperability between side-chains through a semi-stateless governance blockchain whose old data can be safely pruned.

04

Optimized resources through DPoS

By utilizing a EOS-like DPoS consensus mechanism, Nominis aligns optimal incentives through competition and cooperation for the participating nodes. Each coin-owner can vote with the weight of their available holdings for the issues like Block Producers elections for each side-chain, cross-sidechain transactions validation, side-chain spawning and freezing, etc. The awarded reputation points from the cross-sidechain txs validation are an essential part of the final score in the BP election mechanism.

05

No transaction fees

Unlike Ethereum where transaction fees are an irreplaceable part of the mechanism to determine transactions priority, Nominis utilizes the EOS-like practice of staking.
